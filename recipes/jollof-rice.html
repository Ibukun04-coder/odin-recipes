Jollof rice Recipe

Home

Description

Jollof Rice is a beloved West African one-pot dish made with rice simmered in a rich blend of tomatoes, onions, and peppers, infused with aromatic spices. Known for its vibrant orange-red color and bold, smoky-sweet flavor. Each country has its twist, but the Nigerian vrsion is famously party-perfect.

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • Long-grain parboiled rice
  • Fresh tomatoes (blended)
  • Tomato paste
  • Red bell peppers
  • Scotch bonnet peppers
  • Onions
  • Vegetable oil
  • Chicken or beef stock

Seasonings & Spices

  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Curry powder
  • Thyme
  • Bay leaves

Steps

  1. Blend fresh tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, and one onion into a smooth paste and set aside.
  2. Wash the rice thoroughly. Parboil the rice for about 5 minutes, then rinse and set aside.
  3. Heat vegetabke oil in a large pot. Add chopped onions and saute until fragrant. Stir in tomato paste and fry for 3-5 minutes. Add the blended pepper mix and fry on medium heat.
  4. Add salt, seasoning cubes, curry powder, thyme, and bay leaves. Pour in the chicken or beef stock and stir well
  5. Add the parboiled rice and so every grain is coated with sauce. Add enough water or stock to slightly cover the rice.
  6. Allow to cook for 20-30 minutes with stirring too much.
  7. Serve hot with fried plantains, grilled chicken, beef, or fish.
